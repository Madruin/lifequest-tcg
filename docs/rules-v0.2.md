# LifeQuest - Core Rules Document (v0.2)

## Overview

LifeQuest is a non-violent, values-driven trading card game where players build meaningful communities by strategically playing cards and competing over shared resources. The game replaces combat with *progress-based competition*, emphasizing synergy, resource scarcity, and thoughtful interaction.

## Objective

Accumulate **30 Life Points (LP)** by playing People, Locations, and Events that align with your chosen strategies and values. The first player to reach 30 LP at the end of a Winter phase wins.

---

## Components

- A 30-40 card deck per player
- 10 shared **Resource Tokens** (2 of each Value)
- Life Point (LP) tracker or tokens
- Optional: Status tokens, reminder counters

---

## The Five Shared Resources (Values)

Each game begins with a shared pool of **10 Resource Tokens** — 2 of each Value:

| Resource | Identity | Mechanical Theme |
|----------|----------|-----------------|
| **Growth** | Expansion, ambition, self-advancement | Engine building, tempo, "pay now, gain later" |
| **Sustainability** | Balance, renewal, long-term thinking | Recursion, recovery, slow accumulation |
| **Community** | Fairness, inclusion, mutual aid, shared action | Shared effects, structure, collective benefit |
| **Expression** | Creativity, identity, passion | Burst plays, combos, high risk/high reward |
| **Learning** | Knowledge, planning, strategy | Card draw, selection, tutoring, information |

### Resource Economy

- **Claimed tokens** belong to the player who claimed them. They do not automatically return to the pool.
- **Spent tokens** (used to pay card costs) return to the shared pool immediately.
- Tokens only re-enter the pool through spending, card effects, or Bartering. There is no automatic Winter reset.
- The shared pool is a **commons** — resource scarcity and competition over the pool is the primary form of player interaction.

---

## Card Types

### People (Interact)

People are your active agents. They have a **Ready/Exhausted** state.

- When **Ready**, a Person can be **activated**: exhaust them (turn sideways) to use their activated ability. This is a Summer action.
- People **refresh** (return to Ready) at the start of each Spring.
- People may also have **triggered abilities** that fire automatically during specific seasons.
- When multiple triggers resolve in the same window, the **Start Player's triggers resolve first**.

People force tactical decisions every turn: *Do I activate this Person now, or hold them Ready for later?*

### Locations (Develop)

Locations are persistent infrastructure. They provide passive, always-on effects.

- Locations do **not** exhaust. Their effects are always active.
- Locations may have a **Development** track with multiple levels. A Location enters play at Level 1 and can **advance** when conditions are met (typically checked in Autumn).
- Higher levels unlock stronger effects or additional abilities.
- Some card effects or resource conditions can cause a Location to **degrade** (lose a level). A Location that degrades below Level 1 is discarded.
- Locations amplify People and Events. They are the stage; People are the actors.

Locations reward long-term investment: *Do I keep developing this Location or pivot to something new?*

### Events (Disrupt)

Events are one-time effects that reshape the board state.

- Events resolve immediately when played, then go to the discard pile.
- Events can generate LP, draw cards, return tokens to the pool, interact with other players' resources, and more.
- Events are the primary way to inject tempo and respond to changing conditions.

---

## Tags

Cards may have **Tags** (e.g., Artist, Gamer, Convention, Duluth, Nature) that enable synergies. Many abilities reference Tags:

- "Each People card you control with the Artist tag gains +1 LP."
- "If you control a Location with the Convention tag, draw 1 card."

Tags create archetypal identities and reward themed deckbuilding.

---

## Game Setup

1. Place **10 Resource Tokens** in the center of the table: 2 Growth, 2 Sustainability, 2 Community, 2 Expression, 2 Learning.
2. Each player shuffles their deck and draws **5 cards**.
3. Randomly determine the **Start Player** for Year 1.
4. Begin in **Spring of Year 1**.

---

## Turn Structure - The Four Seasons

Each game year consists of four sequential phases. Both players participate in each season before moving to the next.

### SPRING (Refresh & Acquire)

The Start Player acts first, then the other player. Each player does the following in order:

1. **Refresh** all your exhausted People (return them to Ready).
2. **Draw** 1 card from your deck.
3. **Take 1 resource action** — choose one:
   - **Forage**: Claim 1 token from the shared pool.
   - **Barter**: Return 1 of your tokens to the shared pool, then claim 2 tokens of different types from the pool.
   - **Pressure**: Give 2 of your tokens to an opponent, then take 1 token of your choice from their supply.

**Forage** is the safe default — net +1 token, no risk. **Barter** is equally efficient (net +1) but lets you reshape your resources and puts something back into circulation. **Pressure** costs you (net -1) but lets you take exactly what you need from an opponent's hoard. Each is situationally best:

- **Forage**: Early game, when you have nothing to spare.
- **Barter**: Mid-game, when you've accumulated the wrong types.
- **Pressure**: Late game, when an opponent is hoarding a critical resource.

### SUMMER (Action & Play)

The **Second Player acts first** in Summer (compensating for the Start Player's advantage in Spring).

Players alternate taking actions, one at a time. On your action, choose one:

- **Play a card** from your hand (pay its resource cost — spent tokens return to the shared pool).
- **Activate a Person** (exhaust a Ready Person to use their activated ability).
- **Pass**.

After each action, priority passes to the other player. **When both players pass consecutively, the action phase ends.**

If you pass but your opponent takes an action, you get another chance to act. The phase only ends when neither player wants to do anything more.

After the action phase ends:
- **Summer triggers** on all in-play cards resolve. Start Player's triggers resolve first.

**There is no limit on the number of cards you can play.** Resources are the natural gate. If you've built an engine that lets you play four cards in one Summer, that's the payoff.

### AUTUMN (Harvest & Development)

1. **Autumn triggers** on all in-play cards resolve. Start Player's triggers resolve first.
2. **Location Development**: Locations that meet their advancement conditions advance one level.

Autumn is the harvest phase — your board bears fruit, and Locations grow stronger. No cards are played in Autumn.

### WINTER (Reflection & Scoring)

Winter is resolved simultaneously by both players.

1. **Tally LP** from all in-play cards. Add LP gains to each player's total.
2. **Winter triggers** resolve. Start Player's triggers resolve first.
3. **Check win condition**: If any player has **30 or more LP**, the game ends (see Winning the Game).
4. **Hand limit**: Discard down to **5 cards** if over the hand limit.
5. **Start Player passes**: The other player becomes Start Player for the next year.
6. **Advance** to the next Year (return to Spring).

**Important**: Unspent tokens do NOT return to the pool. You keep what you've claimed. The only ways tokens re-enter the shared pool are: spending them on cards, Bartering, or specific card effects.

---

## Winning the Game

- At the end of any **Winter phase**, if a player has **30 or more LP**, they win.
- If both players exceed 30 LP in the same Winter, the player with more total LP wins.
- In the case of a tie, play one additional Year.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Ready** | A Person that has not been activated this year. Can use activated abilities. |
| **Exhausted** | A Person that has been activated. Cannot use activated abilities until refreshed. |
| **Activate** | Exhaust a Ready Person to use their activated ability. This is a Summer action. |
| **Refresh** | Return an Exhausted Person to Ready state. Happens in Spring. |
| **Development Level** | A Location's current power level. Locations enter at Level 1 and may advance or degrade. |
| **Advance** | Increase a Location's Development Level by 1 (checked in Autumn). |
| **Degrade** | Decrease a Location's Development Level by 1. Below Level 1 = discarded. |
| **Forage** | Spring action: claim 1 token from pool. |
| **Barter** | Spring action: return 1 of your tokens, claim 2 different from pool. |
| **Pressure** | Spring action: give 2 of your tokens to opponent, take 1 of theirs. |
| **Start Player** | The player who acts first in Spring and Autumn. Alternates each year. |
| **Tags** | Keywords on cards that enable synergy (e.g., Artist, Gamer, Convention). |
| **Commons / Shared Pool** | The central pool of Resource Tokens that all players draw from. |

## Custom Leagues & Events

- Local leagues may define their own starting Resource Pools (e.g., Duluth League begins with an extra Community token).
- Events can feature unique draft rules, shared decks, or community-built resource maps.
- The Excalibur Con 2026 set is designed as a standalone draft-and-play experience.

---

## Design Principles

- LifeQuest values collaboration, thoughtful planning, and strategic storytelling.
- Cards and resources are designed for accessibility and local flavor.
- There is no combat — only choices, consequences, and shared futures.
- Player interaction is driven by **resource scarcity**, not destruction.
- The shared pool is a **commons**: how you use it says something about the kind of community you're building.
