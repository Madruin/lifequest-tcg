Learn LifeQuest

A step-by-step guide to the game

Welcome to LifeQuest!

LifeQuest is a non-violent, values-driven trading card game where you build meaningful communities by strategically playing cards and competing over shared resources.

The game is set in Duluth, Minnesota and celebrates what makes the Northland special.

Your Goal

Reach 30 Life Points

at the end of a Winter phase to win the game

There is no combat — only choices, consequences, and shared futures. Player interaction is driven by resource scarcity, not destruction.

Each player brings a 30-40 card deck. Let's learn how to play!

The Five Resources

The game begins with a shared pool of 10 tokens in the center — 2 of each resource type:

The pool is a commons — both players claim tokens from it and compete over scarce resources.

Key rule: When you spend tokens to play cards, they return to the shared pool. But tokens you hold stay with you. There is no automatic reset!

This means resource scarcity is the main way players interact. Hoarding tokens denies them from your opponent.

Spring: Refresh & Acquire

Spring → Summer → Autumn → Winter

Each year starts with Spring. The Start Player goes first. Each player does three things:

  1. Refresh all your exhausted People (turn them upright)
  2. Draw 1 card from your deck
  3. Take one resource action:

Forage (Safe Default)

Shared Pool → take 1 → Your Tokens

Net +1 token. Low risk.

Barter (Reshape)

Return 1 → Pool → take 2 different → You

Net +1. Swap colors you don't need.

Pressure (Targeted)

Give 2 to Opponent → Take 1 of Theirs

Net -1 for you, but steal exactly what you need.

Summer: Action & Play

Spring → Summer → Autumn → Winter

Summer is the main action phase! The Second Player acts first (to balance Spring advantage).

Players alternate taking one action at a time. On your turn, choose one:

  • Play a card from your hand — pay its resource cost (spent tokens return to pool)
  • Activate a Person — exhaust a Ready Person to use their ability
  • Pass

When does Summer end? When both players pass in a row. If you pass but your opponent acts, you get another chance!

After the action phase ends, Summer triggers on cards resolve (Start Player's triggers resolve first). There's no limit on cards you can play — resources are the natural gate.

Autumn: Harvest & Development

Spring → Summer → Autumn → Winter

Autumn is the harvest phase. Your board bears fruit and Locations grow stronger. No cards are played in Autumn.

  1. Autumn triggers on in-play cards resolve (Start Player's first)
  2. Location Development check — Locations that meet their advancement conditions go up one level!

Development Example: A Location at Level 1 might say "Advance if you control 2+ People with the Artist tag." If you do, it advances to Level 2, unlocking a stronger passive effect!

Locations reward long-term investment. Do you keep developing this Location or pivot to something new?

Winter: Scoring & Victory

Spring → Summer → Autumn → Winter

Winter is resolved simultaneously by both players:

  1. Tally LP — add LP from all your in-play cards to your total
  2. Winter triggers resolve
  3. Win check — if anyone has 30+ LP, they win!
  4. Discard down to 5 cards in hand
  5. Swap Start Player and begin a new Year

Important: Unspent tokens do NOT return to the pool. You keep what you've claimed! The only ways tokens re-enter the pool are: spending them on cards, Bartering, or card effects.

If both players exceed 30 LP, the player with more total LP wins. Tie? Play one more Year!

Tags & Synergies

Cards have tags that create powerful synergies when you build around them:

Example tags: Artist, Gamer, Convention, Duluth, Nature, Music, Food, Education

Artist Gamer Convention Duluth Nature Music

Many card abilities reference tags:

  • "Each People card you control with the Artist tag gains +1 LP."
  • "If you control a Location with the Convention tag, draw 1 card."

Tags create archetypal identities and reward themed deckbuilding. A deck focused on Artist cards will have different strengths than one built around Convention cards.

Tip: The two starter decks each focus on different resource pairs and tag themes. Try both to see which style suits you!

Ready to Play!

You now know the basics of LifeQuest. Time to jump in!

Remember:

  • Reach 30 LP at end of Winter to win
  • 5 shared resources — scarcity is the main interaction
  • Spring: Refresh, Draw, Resource action
  • Summer: Play cards & activate People
  • Autumn: Triggers + Location Development
  • Winter: Score LP and check for victory

Card abilities are read-and-resolve — read the text and apply it manually. Don't worry about memorizing everything!
