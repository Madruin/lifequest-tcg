Rules Reference

Quick Reference

Goal: Reach 30 LP at end of Winter to win.

Resources (shared pool): Growth (gray), Sustainability (green), Community (yellow), Expression (red), Learning (blue) — 2 each = 10 total

Card Types: People (activate/exhaust), Locations (develop levels), Events (one-time)

Seasons:

  • Spring: Refresh People, Draw 1, Resource action (Forage/Barter/Pressure)
  • Summer: Alternate actions — play card or activate Person. Both pass = end.
  • Autumn: Each plays 1 card. Location Development check.
  • Winter: Tally LP, check 30 LP win, discard to 5.

Resource Actions (Spring):

  • Forage: Take 1 token from shared pool (net +1)
  • Barter: Return 1 of yours, take 2 different from pool (net +1, reshapes resources)
  • Pressure: Give 2 to opponent, take 1 of theirs (net -1, targeted)
Overview & Objective

LifeQuest is a non-violent, values-driven trading card game where players build meaningful communities by strategically playing cards and competing over shared resources.

Accumulate 30 Life Points (LP) by playing People, Locations, and Events. The first player to reach 30 LP at the end of a Winter phase wins.

Components: A 30-40 card deck per player, 10 shared Resource Tokens (2 of each), LP tracker.

Economy: Claimed tokens belong to you. Spent tokens return to pool immediately. No automatic Winter reset. Resource scarcity is the primary form of player interaction.

Card Types

People (Interact): Active agents with Ready/Exhausted state. Activate (exhaust) to use their ability in Summer. Refresh in Spring. May have Initiative for resolution order.

Locations (Develop): Persistent infrastructure with passive, always-on effects. Do not exhaust. May have Development tracks — enter at Level 1, advance in Autumn when conditions are met.

Events (Disrupt): One-time effects that resolve immediately, then discard. Primary way to inject tempo and respond to changing conditions.

Tags: Keywords (Artist, Gamer, Convention, Duluth, etc.) that enable synergies between cards.

Spring Phase

Start Player acts first, then the other player. Each player:

  1. Refresh all exhausted People (return to Ready)
  2. Draw 1 card from deck
  3. Resource action — choose one:
    • Forage: Claim 1 token from shared pool
    • Barter: Return 1 of your tokens, claim 2 different types from pool
    • Pressure: Give 2 of your tokens to opponent, take 1 of theirs
Summer Phase

Second Player acts first (compensating for Start Player's Spring advantage).

Players alternate taking one action at a time:

  • Play a card from hand (pay resource cost — spent tokens return to pool)
  • Activate a Person (exhaust a Ready Person for their ability)
  • Pass

When both players pass consecutively, the action phase ends. Then Summer triggers resolve (Start Player's first, by Initiative order).

No limit on cards played — resources are the natural gate.

Autumn Phase

Start Player acts first, then the other player.

  1. Each player may play 1 card from hand (pay cost). This is the reaction window.
  2. Autumn triggers resolve (Start Player's first).
  3. Location Development: Check advancement conditions for all Locations. Locations that meet conditions advance one level.
Winter & Winning

Winter is resolved simultaneously:

  1. Tally LP from all in-play cards
  2. Winter triggers resolve
  3. Win check: If any player has 30+ LP, game ends
  4. Discard down to 5 cards
  5. Swap Start Player and advance to next Year

Winning: If both exceed 30 LP, higher total wins. Tie = play one more Year.

Important: Unspent tokens do NOT return to pool. You keep what you've claimed.
